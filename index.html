The reality

What keeps fund principals up at night

01 / Liquidity

Most of what you are worth, you cannot spend.

GP commitments. Carry. Founder equity. LP positions that mark once a quarter and settle once a decade. The statement is long. The account you can wire from is short.

Financial planning tools built for salaried executives do not account for J-curve timing, clawback risk, or the difference between a $30M carry estimate and a $9M after-tax distribution four years from now.

02 / Concentration

Everyone close to you is paid by the thing you need to diversify.

You built the fund. You raised the capital. You sit on the IC. The asset you know best is also the asset that dominates your personal exposure. Traditional diversification advice assumes you can sell. You cannot.

The question is how to build a balance sheet around a position you are required to hold.

03 / Velocity

You went from carried interest on paper to eight figures in a single distribution.

The plan is still a draft. Fund II returned 2.8x. Fund III is tracking above that. In five years you went from a mid-seven-figure net worth to something that requires institutional-grade structuring.

Estate documents are outdated. The holding company was set up for tax efficiency in a jurisdiction that has since changed its rules. Cash is sitting in a current account because the last three months were consumed by fundraising.

Anonymized outcomes

Three real situations.

$35M
Case 01 · PE Principal, Singapore

A founding partner of a $4B Asia buyout fund needed to meet a $28M GP commitment to Fund IV. The timing overlapped with a residential purchase in Hong Kong. Selling existing fund positions was not an option.

We structured a $35M credit facility secured against LP interests in Funds II and III, timed to match the Fund IV capital call sequence. The facility was in place within 22 days.

$38M
Case 02 · Hedge Fund Manager, Hong Kong

A portfolio manager with $110M in personal net worth held 70% in a single fund vehicle. No estate plan covered assets in three jurisdictions.

We built a $38M diversified portfolio across investment-grade credit, direct real estate co-investments, and a structured note program. Separately, the wealth planning team established a family trust in New Zealand, consolidating the Hong Kong, UK, and Australian holdings under one structure.

$45M
Case 03 · VC Founder, Southeast Asia

A founding GP received a $45M distribution following a portfolio company's Nasdaq listing. The proceeds arrived in Q4, creating a compressed window for tax-efficient deployment.

We executed a pre-year-end strategy: $12M into a donor-advised fund, $18M into a diversified portfolio with a 60-day funding commitment, and $15M into a revolving credit facility that preserved optionality for a co-investment the GP was evaluating in Q1.
